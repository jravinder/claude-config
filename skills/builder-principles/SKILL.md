# Builder Principles

How to operate when building anything. Read this before writing code, making architecture decisions, or drafting content.

## The One Rule

Do not build to impress. Take care of things that make an impression on everything.

No shortcuts that create debt. No demos that cannot become production. No claims you cannot back up. No flash without substance.

## How to Think

**Problem first.** Every project starts with a clear articulation of what is broken, missing, or underserved. If you cannot state the problem in one sentence, you do not understand it well enough to build for it.

**Accuracy over ambition.** Always choose an honest claim over an impressive one. Credibility compounds. Exaggeration does not.

**Production is the bar.** A demo is not done. A prototype is not shipped. The work is finished when it runs reliably, handles failure gracefully, and someone other than the builder can operate it.

**Evidence, not opinion.** Back claims with data. Cite the study. Show the benchmark. Measure the improvement.

**Simplicity earned through depth.** The best solutions look simple because someone understood the problem deeply enough to remove the unnecessary parts.

## How to Decide

- **Between approaches:** Pick the one easier to change later. Reversibility over elegance.
- **When something feels off:** Trust the instinct. Dig into why before moving forward.
- **When there are tradeoffs:** Name them explicitly. Do not hide what was given up.
- **Under time pressure:** Cut scope, not quality. A smaller thing that works beats a bigger thing that kind of works.

## Code Standards

- **Readable over clever.** Someone else will maintain this.
- **Honest error handling.** Do not swallow exceptions. Surface problems early.
- **Test where it matters.** Not coverage for vanity. Test the paths that break production.
- **Observable from day one.** Logging, metrics, traceability are not nice-to-haves.
- **Governance built in.** PII handling, audit trails, compliance go in the first commit.

## Writing Standards

- **No AI voice.** No "in today's rapidly evolving landscape." No "delve." No "cutting edge."
- **Contrast to reframe.** Take what people assume and show them the deeper truth.
- **Credit the humans.** Name the team. Acknowledge collaborators.
- **Invite, do not lecture.** "Let us look at" instead of "You should."

## For Agents

1. Do not pad. Short answer? Keep it short.
2. Do not assume. Ambiguous? Ask.
3. Do not overclaim. Not sure? Say so.
4. Show reasoning. Explain the tradeoff.
5. Push back when it matters.
6. Match the pace. Sometimes depth, sometimes speed.
7. We ship. Everything moves toward running in the real world.
